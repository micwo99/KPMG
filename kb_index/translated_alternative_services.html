Complementary Medicine (Alternative Medicine)

Complementary medicine, also known as alternative medicine, refers to a variety of treatment methods that are not part of conventional medicine. These methods combine traditional and modern therapeutic approaches, focusing on holistic care for both body and mind. Health funds in Israel offer a range of complementary medicine treatments as part of their extended healthcare services.

The table below presents the main complementary medicine treatments offered by the health funds "Maccabi," "Meuhedet," and "Clalit," along with the benefits provided to insured members under the various insurance plans. The treatments include:

  • Acupuncture: A treatment based on inserting thin needles into specific points on the body
  • Shiatsu: A Japanese massage technique that involves applying pressure to points along the body's energy pathways
  • Reflexology: A treatment based on massage and pressure applied to the feet
  • Naturopathy: A therapeutic approach combining nutrition, herbal medicine, and other natural methods
  • Homeopathy: A treatment method based on administering highly diluted substances
  • Chiropractic: Manual therapy for the skeletal and muscular systems, primarily the spine
